My service work also treats research infrastructure as part of scholarly
practice. I have coordinated cross-institutional working groups on durable data
and software stewardship, developed review checklists that connect published
claims to executable evidence, and mentored researchers who were introducing
testing and documentation into established scientific projects. These efforts
have shown me how much progress depends on making reliable practice teachable,
visible, and proportionate to the resources of the group adopting it.

At Example University I would bring these strands together through a research
programme in transparent computational methods, courses that pair mathematical
reasoning with reproducible implementation, and shared training for students
and collaborators. I am particularly interested in creating project structures
where students can contribute meaningful research while learning how to test,
explain, and preserve their work for readers beyond the immediate team.
}
